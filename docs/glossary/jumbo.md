# Conforming, high-balance, jumbo

> Loan-type categories that determine your rate, your down-payment minimum, and how much paperwork you sign.

A "loan type" sounds technical but it's mostly about **how much principal** you're borrowing relative to a federal cap.

- **Conforming** — under the FHFA national baseline ($806,500 in 2026). Backed by Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac. Best rates, lowest down (5% standard; 3% via HomeReady / Home Possible).
- **High-balance conforming** — between the national baseline and the high-cost-area ceiling ($1,209,750 in every Bay Area county for 2026). Still GSE-backed but priced 0.125–0.25 percentage points higher than plain conforming.
- **Jumbo** — above the high-cost ceiling. Not GSE-backed; lender keeps the loan or sells privately. Usually requires 10–20% down and a stricter credit profile.
- **FHA** — government-insured, 3.5% down (FICO ≥ 580). Subject to mortgage-insurance premium (MIP) for the loan's life if down is < 10%.

**Why the Bay Area is special:** every Bay Area county (Alameda, Contra Costa, Marin, Napa, San Francisco, San Mateo, Santa Clara, Solano, Sonoma) is on FHFA's "high-cost area" list, so the high-balance ceiling — not the national baseline — is what lets you stay in GSE-backed land for prices up to ~$1.2M. Above that, you're in jumbo.

### Concrete example

A $1.5M Pleasanton home with $200K down means a $1.3M loan. That's above Alameda's $1,209,750 high-balance ceiling → **jumbo**. A $1.4M Pleasanton home with $200K down means a $1.2M loan → **high-balance conforming**, with a slightly better rate.

Source: [FHFA 2026 Conforming Loan Limit announcement](https://www.fhfa.gov/news/news-release/fhfa-announces-2026-conforming-loan-limits).
