# Private mortgage insurance (PMI)

> An extra monthly fee you pay until you owe less than 80% of the home's value.

Lenders take on more risk when you borrow more than 80% of a home's price. To offset it, they require **private mortgage insurance** — a monthly premium added on top of your principal & interest.

PMI applies on **conforming and jumbo** loans when your down payment is under 20%. (FHA has its own version, **MIP**, that works similarly but with different rules.)

The premium scales with **credit score** and **loan-to-value (LTV)**:

| FICO band | Annual PMI rate (rough) |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| 760+ | 0.30–0.45% |
| 740–759 | 0.45–0.65% |
| 720–739 | 0.55–0.85% |
| 700–719 | 0.85–1.10% |
| 680–699 | 1.05–1.50% |

Our Phase-1 affordability default is **0.55%/year of the original loan amount**, which lands in the 740–759 mid-range.

PMI **goes away** automatically when you reach 78% LTV based on the original purchase price (per the Homeowners Protection Act of 1998), or you can request cancellation at 80%. Re-appraising after a few years of appreciation is a common way to drop it sooner.

### Concrete example

You buy a $1.0M Fremont condo with $100K down (10% LTV → PMI applies).

- Loan amount: $900K
- PMI rate: 0.55%/year
- Annual PMI: $4,950 → **$413/month**

Three years later, the home appraises at $1.15M and your loan balance is $866K. Your LTV is now $866K / $1.15M = 75%. You request PMI cancellation, and your monthly cost drops by $413. That's why the rate-sensitivity slider in our app shows the PMI line specifically — drop it and you've got a real lever.
