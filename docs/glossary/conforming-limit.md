# Conforming loan limit (2026)

> The cutoff below which Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac will buy your mortgage. In every Bay Area county, the high-cost ceiling is $1,209,750 in 2026.

Each November, the FHFA publishes the next year's conforming loan limits. There are two thresholds that matter for Bay Area buyers:

- **National baseline** — $806,500 in 2026. This is what "conforming" usually means in lender quotes.
- **High-cost ceiling** — 150% of the baseline, **$1,209,750** in 2026. FHFA designates 100+ counties as "high-cost areas," and **all nine Bay Area counties qualify**. Loans between the baseline and this ceiling are "high-balance conforming" — still GSE-backed, slightly more expensive in rate.

Above $1,209,750 you're in **jumbo** territory: typically a 10–20% minimum down, stricter credit profile, and a different lender than the GSE-backed conforming desk.

**Why the threshold matters at the dollar level:** lenders frequently quote rates in 0.125pp steps, and the conforming-vs-high-balance break is one of those steps. A $1.21M loan and a $1.20M loan can carry rates that differ by 0.125–0.25pp — so on a borderline price, **putting an extra $5K down to slip below the threshold can save real money over the loan's life**.

### Concrete example

You're buying a $1.5M Fremont home with $300K down → $1.2M loan → just under high-balance, you're high-balance conforming.

If you put only $200K down → $1.3M loan → that crosses into jumbo territory ($1.3M > $1,209,750), changing your rate, your lender pool, and your closing-cost mix.

Source: [FHFA 2026 Conforming Loan Limit announcement](https://www.fhfa.gov/news/news-release/fhfa-announces-2026-conforming-loan-limits) (published 2025-11-25). Limits are revised annually.
